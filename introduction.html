The Theodicy Problem

Why do bad things happen to good people? Why do monumental events stem from seemingly trivial causes? The JFK assassination confronts us with what theologians call the problem of theodicy—the apparent randomness of suffering and significance in a world we desperately want to believe has meaning and order.

The Ostendorf Parallels: When Coincidence Becomes Cosmic

Lloyd Ostendorf discovered astonishing parallels between the Kennedy and Lincoln assassinations:

  • Lincoln elected to Congress in 1846; Kennedy in 1946
  • Lincoln elected President in 1860; Kennedy in 1960
  • Both shot on Friday, in presence of wives
  • Both succeeded by men named Johnson
  • Andrew Johnson born 1808; Lyndon Johnson born 1908
  • Booth born 1839; Oswald born 1939
  • Both assassins known by three names with 15 letters total

Are we glimpsing hidden architecture of reality, or imposing meaning on meaningless coincidence?
